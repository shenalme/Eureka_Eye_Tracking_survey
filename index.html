Webcam Eye-Tracking Study

We're exploring how people naturally watch film trailers — which moments capture attention, and how that attention moves across the screen. Using your device's webcam, we record where you look while you watch a small set of trailers.

Participant Information Sheet

Everything you need to know, before you begin

Please read each section below. You can expand or collapse them in any order.

Purpose of the study

This study investigates how people naturally look at film trailers — which moments draw the eye, and how attention shifts across a scene over time. Your gaze data will help researchers understand visual attention during film viewing.

What participation involves

After a short webcam calibration step, you will watch 5 film trailers, one at a time, while your eye movements are recorded. A brief questionnaire follows each trailer. See the Study Procedure section above for the full sequence.

Estimated duration

The full session takes approximately 15–20 minutes, including a brief webcam calibration step at the start.

Webcam & eye-tracking requirements

You will need a device with a working webcam and a stable internet connection, in a reasonably well-lit room. Eye position is estimated from the video feed in your browser; no video is required to be permanently stored (see confidentiality below).

Confidentiality & data protection

Your data is linked only to a randomly generated participant ID — never your name or email. Data is stored securely and used solely for research purposes, in line with Cardiff Metropolitan University's data protection policy and applicable data protection law.

Analysis is carried out at the group level on anonymised data only. No individual-level analysis or reporting is performed, and you will not be identifiable in any results.

Risks & benefits

There are no anticipated risks beyond typical screen use. There is no direct personal benefit, but your participation contributes to research on visual attention and film perception.

Voluntary participation

Taking part is entirely voluntary. You may stop at any time without giving a reason and without any penalty or disadvantage.

Study Procedure

What happens when you open the study link

Now that you know what the study is about, here is exactly what to expect once the webcam link opens:

  1. 1
    Calibration. Your webcam briefly calibrates to your eye position so your gaze can be tracked accurately.
  2. 2
    Watch a trailer. You'll be shown a film trailer while your eye movements are recorded in the background.
  3. 3
    Answer a brief questionnaire. A short set of questions follows each trailer.
  4. 4
    Repeat for 5 trailers in total. Steps 2 and 3 repeat until you have watched all five trailers and completed each questionnaire.

Withdrawal Information

You are free to withdraw at any time

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw from the study at any point before completing it, without needing to give a reason and without any penalty.

Because your data is linked only to an anonymous participant ID and not to your name or contact details, please note that you must request withdrawal before you finish the study — once your session ends, your data can no longer be traced back to you for removal. If you wish to withdraw during the study, simply close the browser tab, and no further data will be recorded. To request withdrawal of data already submitted, contact the research team at [research-team@cardiffmet.ac.uk] quoting your participant ID, shown to you at the end of the consent step below, within [withdrawal window, e.g. 14 days] of taking part.
